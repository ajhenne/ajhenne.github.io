ADAM HENNESSY

PhD in High Energy Astrophysics
Software & Data Engineer
Quantitative Specialist

For the past four years, I have worked on gamma-ray busts, the most energetic explosions in the universe, primarily using the Swift space observatory and LOFAR radio interferometry telescope.

Processing terabyte-scale datasets in high-noise environments, and developing an automated predictive modelling pipeline for complex, time-series data has forged a deep expertise in statistical modelling and quantitative analysis.

About Me

While astrophysics will always be a passion of mine, the nature of my work (and perhaps research in general) meant that, at times, it was an interesting wrapper around a lot of programming, data processing and statistical analysis. Thankfully, I am quite passionate about everything data and coding.

Through my PhD, and various projects I've done over the years, I've developed a strong foundation of programming and data-based skills, not limited to pipeline development, data modelling, statistical analysis, and data visualisation. No less within the astrophysics field, where data sources are inherently disparate, complex and noisy.

My work has primarily been centered around Python, with some SQL. Alongside this, I have experience and interest in various other technologies. This includes a bit of C++, and several web development projects, including both static HTML/CSS/JavaScript sites, and full web apps with the Flask framework.
